Crafting with yarn.

A variety of art can be made with yarn. Some using yarn and gluing onto a canvas to create an image. Others for making:

  • Fabrics
  • Blankets
  • Scarves
  • Winter Hats
  • etc.
Some crafts that are not covered on this website are:
Weaving The act of taking a sewing needle to interlace two sets on yarn or threads on a weaving loom to create cloth or fabric. Characterizing the material that is strung on the loom as the warp, and the material being pulled by the needle the weft.
Loom Knitting The act of using a loom instead of traditional knitting needles to make knitted fabric. Loom sizes dictating the size of the fabric. Typically used due to its ease of use and the reduced likelihood of dropping a stitch
Rug Making The act of using a special canvas material in which many techniques, such as braiding, latch hooking, and weaving, to create a carpet or rug.
Macrame The act of tying cords, yarn, or string to create decortive items.
Embroidery The act of using a sewing needle and yarn on a woven campus to create illustrations to embelish an existing fabric
Needlepoint Similar to embroidery, its main difference is the loose weave of the canvas to create an image or pattern
Appliqué The act of sewing multiple layers of fabric to create entricate patterns.
Patchwork The act of ajoining multiple fabrics to create a partern
Quilting Similar to Appliqué and Patchwork, it is the art of making smaller patterns to be ajoined into a larger piece that are sectional in visual
Lacework The act of using a needle or hook to create delicate patterns that is often used for events such as weddings.
