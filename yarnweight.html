Yarn Weight.

Depending on the weight of the yarn it can change the guage of your project and the size tool you will used

Weight 0 or Lace

Also called crochet thread. Typically used for lacework. Typical circumfrence of needles and hooks are 1.5 to 2.5 mm.

Weight 1 or Superfine

Also called fingering, sock, or baby weight yarns. Typical needle and hook circumfrence is 2.5 to 3.5 mm.

Weight 2 or Fine Weight

Also called Double Knit or Light Worsted. Typically is the weight of yarn you see when you see. Typical needle or hook circumfrence used is 3.5 to 4.5 mm.

Weight 3 or Light weight yarn

Also called light weight yarn or Double Knit. Typically is the weight of yarn you see when you see. Typical needle or hook circumfrence used is 4.5 to 5.5 mm.

Weight 4 or Worsted Weight yarn

Most versatile and widley used yarn wieght. Noteably being an equal balance of durability and workability. Typical needle or hook circumfrence used is 5.5 to 6.5 mm.

Weight 5 or Bulky/Chunky yarn

Charactericzed by its thickness and ideal for making fabric or crafts that heat retention is important. Typical needle or hook circumfrence used is 6.5 to 7.5 mm.

Weight 6 or Super Bulky/Chunky yarn

Ideal for quick projects, typically used for blankets. Typical needle or hook circumfrence used is 7.5 to 8.5 mm.

Weight 7 or Jumbo weight yarn

The thickest yarn available, crafts made from this yarn is characterized for being heavy, or weighted. Good for making a weighted blanket. Typical needle or hook circumfrence used is 8.5 to 9.5 mm.

Needle and Hook Sizes

Though you can use other hook or needles sizes than what I have recommended. You need to focus on your guage made with the hook or needle size you use. It helps you determine how much material is needed.

Calculating your guage

To calculate your guage, you need to count how many rows you have in an inch of your width, and how many columns you have height in an inch. You can find more specific instructions within the corcheting sections on this webiste with additional help for identifying your guage. As well as your knitting guage help within knitting sections of this site.
